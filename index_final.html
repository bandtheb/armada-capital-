01    Thesis

Infrastructure that fixes the timing, access, and coordination of money globally.

Money moves badly. It moves late, through the wrong channels, with too much friction and too little transparency. We back the founders building the underlying infrastructure that changes that: settlement rails, compliance architecture, data coordination, and the embedded tooling that makes global financial systems actually work for the people inside them.

We are business model agnostic. What we care about is whether the infrastructure is real, the market is structurally underserved, and the founder understands the regulatory terrain well enough to build something durable.

Our primary focus is pre-obvious companies in this space, at pre-seed and seed. We also invest opportunistically across adjacent regulated markets where the same timing, access, and coordination problems appear.

01
Timing

Settlement delays, payment cycles, and cash flow gaps that punish the people with the least margin for error. Infrastructure that compresses time as a feature, not an afterthought.

02
Access

Regulated markets where the infrastructure assumes a customer who does not exist. New rails, embedded tooling, and compliance architecture built for who actually shows up.

03
Coordination

Multi-party, cross-jurisdiction complexity that legacy systems were never designed to handle. Data infrastructure and orchestration layers that make global financial coordination possible.

03    About

Ilona
Limonta-Volkova

Founder & General Partner

Ilona is a media-native investor with six years covering fintech for Forbes, a Wharton undergraduate degree, and a Kellogg MBA. She has spent her career at the intersection of capital markets, emerging technology, and financial infrastructure, with prior roles at the Hilton Foundation overseeing a global alternatives portfolio, Vanguard corporate development where she led the acquisition of direct indexing platform Just Invest, and in oil and gas private equity.

She founded Bear and the Bull, a newsletter and media brand covering the infrastructure stories shaping financial services, and produces Money Memories, a podcast distributed by NPR affiliate Louisville Public Media. Both serve as sourcing infrastructure: operators, founders, and early customers come through the media before they raise.

She serves as a Venture Partner at Media for Growth and maintains advisory relationships with emerging managers and fintech operators on fund narrative, LP positioning, and go-to-market strategy.
